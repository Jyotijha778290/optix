KEY

Build equity from day one

With Key, you start building home equity from day one, and your investment can grow with real estate. Each month a portion of your monthly payment goes towards building your equity, this starts at $50 and is dependent on the building. You can invest more at any time.

No mortage required

With Key, you are never required to take on a mortgage. You can choose to take one on and purchase your home the traditional way after the third year, but there is no obligation.

Accessible ownership

With Key, you can start owning and building home equity for an initial contribution starting at 2.5% depending on the building, this is around 15k for many of our homes.

No hidden fees

Key fees are only 1% of your invested equity (as little as 0.025% of your homes value) plus a one-time onboarding fee of $250. If you choose to take on a mortgage after the third year, you will pay the traditional costs associated with buying and selling real estate.

Rent-to-own

Not building equity right away

With rent-to-own models, you start building equity once your lease period is over and you decide to purchase the home.

Get mortgage ready

With rent-to-own, when your lease agreement runs out, you need to qualify for a mortgage. If you decide to not take on a mortgage and purchase the home, you risk losing all of the equity youve been saving and your initial deposit.

Requires large downpayment

With rent-to-own once your lease agreement runs out you are required to put 5-20% down.

Extra upfront fees

With rent-to-own models youre on the hook for non-refundable upfront fees that typically range between 1-5% of the purchase price..

KEY

It starts at 2.5%

To become a co-owner with Key, the initial minimum home equity investment starts from 2.5% of the value of your home depending on the building, which will be around $15k for many.

No mortgage required

With Key, you are never required to take on a mortgage. You can choose to do so after the third year but there is no obligation, you can choose to continuing co-owning and building home equity at your own pace.

Increased flexibility

Giving you the option of continuing to co-own or take on a mortgage after three years is one way Key provides flexibility.

Hassle free exit

When you're ready to move out, just give 75-days notice (after the first year), and you'll get back your home equity investments plus your portion of the appreciation. And you'll save most of the typical selling costs.

Traditional ownership

Typically 20%

Traditional homeownership requires 5-20% down depending on the value of your home.

Required mortgage

With traditional homeownership you are required to qualify for and take on a mortgage. A mortgage also means taking on the risk of rising interest rates.

Locked in

With traditional homeownership you are typically locked into a 20-year mortgage that can be difficult to exit should you need to move or if your circumstances change.

Obstructive exit process

Depending on your mortgage you could be looking at expensive mortgage breakage fees, and with a down payment that can easily tie up most of your money you may risk becoming house poor.

KEY

An equity stake from day one

With Key, for an inital contribution that starts at just 2.5% you have an equity position starting from day one, that can grow in value over time. You can build equity at your own pace and invest more at any time.

Monthly payments can decrease

With Key, each month a portion of your monthly payment will go towards building your home equity. This starts at $50 and is dependent on the building, but you can invest more as well. The more of the home you own the less your monthly payment will be.

More security of tenancy

With co-ownership, both co-owners are signed to a predetermined term. This assures security of tenancy and predictable monthly payments. At the end of your term you have the option to buy your home but it is not required.

A home you can make your own

Being an Owner-Resident with Key means that you have a home that you can really make your own. Whether its a new paint colour for your walls or new kitchen cabinets, we encourage all of our Owner-Residents to personalize to suit them.

Renting

No equity stake

With renting, youre not building any equity but rather paying a lump sum each month that could be going towards your landlords mortgage.

Rent will only increase

.With renting, your rent will only ever increase, not decrease.

Dreaded 60-day notice

With renting, the security of tenancy is limited since there is always the possibility of that dreaded 60-day notice to move out.

Limited personalizations

With renting, there are typically limitations around the personalizations youre able to make in your home, as most landlords want to the unit to remain the same when you move out as it was when you moved in.
